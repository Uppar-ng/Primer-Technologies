About Zaure

We are building a trusted space where property owners and seekers meet directly — without agents, hidden fees, or middlemen.

Our Story

Zaure was born from a clear market failure: in Northern Nigeria, finding a home meant navigating a fragmented system of intermediaries who controlled access, distorted pricing, and offered little accountability.

Property owners lacked a direct channel to reach serious tenants and buyers. Seekers were routinely asked to pay "viewing fees" before seeing a single room.

We built Zaure to close that gap.

Named after the traditional Hausa entrance hall—the zaure—our platform serves as the trusted threshold where verified owners and genuine seekers meet. We provide the secure infrastructure and the verification layer; the transaction remains entirely between the parties.

Behind Zaure is Alamin Ahmed, a passionate advocate for digital inclusion and transparent marketplaces in Northern Nigeria. As Founder & CEO, Alamin brings a deep understanding of the local housing landscape and a commitment to leveraging technology for social impact. Under his leadership, Zaure is scaling across Kano, Kaduna, Zaria, and Abuja, driven by a team dedicated to making property discovery transparent, equitable, and human-centered.
